# Design Playbook

## Goal

Make slides look intentional. Avoid a generic AI deck aesthetic.

## Core principles

- Choose one dominant color, not three equal colors.
- Vary layouts across the deck instead of repeating the same bullet page.
- Use size contrast aggressively: titles must feel clearly larger than body text.
- Give every slide a visual anchor.
- Leave breathing room. Empty space is part of the design.

## Typography

Good pairings:

- Georgia + Calibri
- Cambria + Aptos
- Trebuchet MS + Calibri
- Palatino Linotype + Garamond

Target sizes:

- Title: 36-44 pt
- Section label: 20-24 pt
- Body: 14-18 pt
- Caption or source: 10-12 pt

## Layout patterns

Rotate among a few reliable patterns:

- Two-column explainer: argument on one side, image or chart on the other
- Metric cards: 2x2 or 2x3 grids for stats or themes
- Timeline or process strip
- Full-bleed image with a high-contrast text panel
- Comparison slide with mirrored left/right sections
- Section divider with a short statement and strong background contrast

## Spacing rules

- Keep at least 0.5 in outer margin.
- Keep 0.3-0.5 in gaps between related blocks.
- If a slide feels crowded, remove content before shrinking everything.

## Avoid

- Centering long paragraphs
- Using the same layout for every slide
- Weak contrast between background and text
- Decorative lines added under every title
- Text-only content slides when a chart, icon row, or card layout would work better
- Placeholder icons or stock imagery that does not fit the topic
